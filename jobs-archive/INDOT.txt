About the Job

The Geologist works with the Testing Engineer, District Testing personnel, other divisions and departments to report findings and make recommendations of construction material aggregates and soils for highway construction. Incumbent researches/investigates aggregates incorporated into construction contracts that do not comply with specifications. This position reports directly to the Highway Engineer Supervisor in Testing.
A Day in the Life

    Administers the Certified Aggregate Producers Program (CAPP) at the District to ensure compliance with department specifications and policies;
    Schedules and attends CAPP Audits, and monitors the quality status of all approved aggregate sources in the District;
    Reviews Annual Aggregate Source reports, Summary of Ledge Quality reports and Summary of Production Quality reports;
    Identifies and defines the stratigraphy of crushed stone quarries that furnish aggregate to the Indiana Department of Transportation (INDOT) for contracts and purchase orders;
    Inspects, identifies, samples, and describes ledge and core samples to determine the stratigraphic and lithologic composition of rock materials;
    Instructs Highway Technician classes;
    Performs other related duties as assigned.

What We're Looking For

    Bachelors degree in geology with emphasis on petrography, sedimentology, and stratigraphy'
    Licensed Professional Geologist is preferred (those without LPG may be underslotted);

What You'll Need For Success

    Considerable knowledge of the geology in Indiana;
    Limited knowledge of American Association of State Highway Transportation (AASHTO), American Society for Testing Materials (ASTM), Indiana Test Methods, and INDOT specifications;
    Broad knowledge of, and ability to apply, state specifications, programs, guidelines, manuals, and regulations relative to highway construction and materials testing;
    Working knowledge of, and ability to operate, geologic field instruments and photographic equipment;
    Working knowledge of survey and mapping techniques;
    Ability to read and interpret a variety of contract documents including plans and proposals, specifications and special provisions;
    Ability to read, interpret and write geologic descriptions and reports;
    Considerable knowledge in Outlook, Word, Excel, and Access programs;
    Must possess a valid driver’s license;
    Ability to work in rugged terrain and under adverse weather conditions;
    Ability to work and effectively communicate with others both verbally and in writing;
    Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts and draw valid conclusions, and the ability to understand technical manuals and/or geologic map data, collate, and classify geologic
    information.
